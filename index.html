What is OSINT?

Open Source Intelligence is the art and science of collecting and analyzing information from publicly available sources to produce actionable intelligence.

Public Data
Analysis
Verification

Why It Matters

Defenders

Security teams use OSINT for threat intelligence, identifying leaked credentials, and understanding their own digital footprint before attackers do.

Investigators

Journalists, law enforcement, and private investigators use it to track missing persons, verify facts, and uncover corruption.

Attackers

Cybercriminals use OSINT for reconnaissance (Recon) to craft highly targeted phishing emails and find vulnerable infrastructure.

SOCMINT (Social Media Intel)

Analyzing connections, posts, and geolocated photos. Investigators look for slips in operational security (OpSec) in daily posts.

  • Username reuse across platforms
  • Tagged locations in photos
  • Family connections & networks

Infrastructure Recon

Mapping the digital footprint of a target. Subdomains, IP addresses, and technology stacks tell a story of vulnerability.

Two Sides of the Coin

The Attacker

"How can I exploit this information?"

  • Finding employees for phishing targets
  • Locating exposed databases
  • Profiling physical security via photos

The Researcher

"How can I verify or protect this?"

  • Identifying fake news campaigns
  • Removing sensitive data from public view
  • Verifying user identities

Real-World Impact

Tracking Global Events

Using geolocation on TikTok and Telegram videos to map troop movements and verify news reports in conflict zones.

Identifying Catfish

Reverse image searching profile pictures to expose fraudulent accounts and romance scams, protecting victims from financial loss.

Exposed Servers

Researchers finding unsecured S3 buckets containing millions of customer records and notifying companies before hackers find them.

Key Takeaways

  • It's Powerful & Legal: Information is out there; the skill lies in finding and interpreting it.
  • Dual Use: The same tools used to catch criminals are used by criminals to target victims.
  • Context is King: Data points are meaningless without the pattern that connects them.

“OSINT is not about secret hacking tricks—it’s about connecting the dots in plain sight.”
